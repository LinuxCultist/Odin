Pizza

Description

Pizza is a baked dish made with a flatbread crust topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings like meats or vegetables. Originating in Italy, it’s now one of the most popular foods worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough
  • Tomato sauce
  • Pepperoni slices
  • Olive oil
  • Herbs

How To Make It — Step By Step Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven: Heat to 475–500°F (245–260°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it heats (30 minutes if possible).

  2. Prepare your work surface: Lightly flour or dust with cornmeal so the dough won’t stick.

  3. Shape the dough: Press the dough ball from the center outward with your fingers, stretching into a roughly 12" circle. Use a rolling pin if you prefer — leave a slightly thicker rim for the crust.
  4. Move to a peel or pan: ransfer the stretched dough to a lightly floured pizza peel or an inverted baking sheet (or a greased baking sheet).

  5. Top it: Brush the rim with a little olive oil. Spread the sauce evenly, leaving the outer rim bare. Sprinkle mozzarella over the sauce, then arrange pepperoni slices on top. Finish with a light sprinkle of oregano if using.

  6. Bake: Slide the pizza onto the preheated stone or place the baking sheet in the oven. Bake at 475°F for about 8–12 minutes (or at 500°F for about 6–9 minutes) until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly with small brown spots.

  7. Finish: If you want extra browning, broil for 30–60 seconds watching closely. Remove when done.

  8. Rest and slice: Let the pizza rest 2–4 minutes, then slice and serve.
